FDA & Regulatory Activity
Spryte Medical nOCT Imaging System
New FDA IDE
First FDA-approved clinical trial of an intravascular optical imaging system designed specifically for the neurovasculature. The nOCT system uses high-resolution optical coherence tomography to provide micron-level image resolution of intracranial vessel walls and therapeutic devices during aneurysm treatment. The INSYTE pivotal trial will evaluate safety and effectiveness.
Enrollment: Early 2026

GTx-104 Injectable Nimodipine
FDA Decision
Injectable formulation of nimodipine for patients with aneurysmal subarachnoid hemorrhage. Designed for peripheral IV use as an aqueous formulation, replacing the current oral nimodipine regimen which can be difficult to administer in critically ill patients. FDA decision expected in 2026.

FRED X Flow Diverter: Multicenter US Study Results
Study Device
Largest US series documenting the FRED X flow diverter's efficacy, safety, and long-term outcomes. The device incorporates innovative X technology surface coating to reduce platelet adhesion and thrombogenicity. Patients treated between Feb 2022 and Feb 2024 at 6 high-volume centers.

Intrasaccular Flow Disruptors
Device
A newer class of devices placed inside the aneurysm sac rather than across the parent vessel. Eliminates the need for parent-vessel deployment, reducing risks associated with traditional flow diversion. Particularly promising for wide-necked bifurcation aneurysms.

ClearPath Nasal Balloon Catheter System
New Device Technique
A precision-guided nasal balloon catheter designed to gently reposition targeted nasal structures without extensive tissue trauma. Incisionless Septoplasty
Technique
Uses high-definition endoscopic cameras and specialized instruments for precise correction with minimal impact on surrounding tissues. Inflatable dilators gently expand nasal passages for improved access and visibility. No external incisions, reduced recovery time, and preservation of natural nasal structure.

Surgical Technique Advances
Modified Extracorporeal Septoplasty
Study Technique
Prospective study showing improved quality of life and aesthetics with a low complication rate. Modified maneuvers preserve the philosophy of extracorporeal septoplasty while reducing technical complexity, decreasing surgical time, and eliminating the need for drills. Keystone preservation reduces risk of dorsal destabilization.
NOSE score reduction: -42.0
Residual deviation: 7.4%

Sling-Bridge Technique
New Technique
A novel technique in extracorporeal septorhinoplasty that introduces a new method for stabilizing the reconstructed septum. Published in Frontiers in Surgery (2024), representing an evolution in how surgeons approach complex septal deformities involving the L-strut.
2024 Frontiers →
Septoplasty vs. Non-Surgical: Meta-Analysis
Meta-Analysis
A 2025 systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials found that septoplasty significantly improved NOSE scores compared to non-surgical interventions at 6 and 12 months, though no notable differences at 3 months. Strengthens the evidence base for surgical intervention in moderate-to-severe cases.

3D-Printed PCL Nasal Septal Implants
Device Study
Composite microporous polycaprolactone (PCL) nasal implants created via 3D printing demonstrate long-term clinical efficacy and safety. The implants provide proper mechanical support with excellent biocompatibility and surgical manipulability. Patient-specific designs enable precise fit for individual septal anatomy.

Bioabsorbable PLA/PCL Mesh Splints
Device
3D-printed bioabsorbable nasal splints made from PLA or PCL that degrade after 2-3 years, providing sufficient support time post-surgery. Used as inner splints to prevent secondary deformation, with simple design allowing easy insertion between septal cartilage and perichondrium without specific complications.
