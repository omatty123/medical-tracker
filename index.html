New FDA IDE
First FDA-approved clinical trial of an intravascular optical imaging system for the neurovasculature. Uses optical coherence tomography for micron-level resolution of vessel walls and therapeutic devices during aneurysm treatment. The INSYTE pivotal trial will evaluate safety and effectiveness.

Injectable formulation of nimodipine for aneurysmal subarachnoid hemorrhage. Designed for peripheral IV use, replacing the oral regimen which is difficult to administer in critically ill patients. FDA decision expected 2026.

Surpass Evolve (Stryker): SEASE Post-Market Study
Study Device
SEASE post-market study of 305 patients (332 aneurysms). Complete occlusion: 73.0% at 10.2 months. Major stroke rate: 2%. Procedure-related mortality: 0.7%. Confirms safety profile of this newer-generation flow diverter.

SILK Vista with P8RI Anti-Thrombogenic Coating
Preclinical Device
Preclinical study of a P8RI peptide coating (mimicking CD31 protein) on the SILK Vista flow diverter (Balt). Showed significant reduction in fibrin binding and platelet adhesion, potentially reducing the need for aggressive dual antiplatelet therapy.

WEB Device (Intrasaccular Flow Disruption)
FDA Approved Device
Only FDA-approved intrasaccular device. Placed inside the aneurysm sac rather than across the parent vessel. WEB-IT trial: adequate occlusion 84.6% at 1 year, 76.8% sustained at 5 years. Particularly useful for wide-necked bifurcation aneurysms where flow diverters are not ideal.
5-YEAR DATA MDPI →
Contour Neurovascular System
IDE Trial Device
Dual-layered nitinol mesh forming a bowl-shaped hemisphere at the aneurysm neck. Treats aneurysm necks 2-10.5mm. The US IDE NECC study is currently underway, potentially offering an alternative to the WEB device for intrasaccular treatment.
ENROLLING PubMed →

2025 systematic review identified 27 prototype bioresorbable flow diverters across 17 studies. Materials include poly-L-lactic acid, polycaprolactone, and biodegradable iron-manganese-nitrogen metals in hybrid configurations. In vivo: 61% achieved 80%+ sac occlusion, 33% complete occlusion.

Management Guidelines & Screening
ARISE I Consensus Review on Intracranial Aneurysm Management
Landmark Consensus
Major expert consensus published in Stroke (AHA, May 2024). Endorsed endovascular therapy as mainstay for ruptured aneurysms based on robust RCT data. Updated screening, watchful waiting, and treatment decision frameworks. Oral nimodipine remains the only pharmacologic therapy proven to improve SAH outcomes.
MAY 2024 AHA Journals →

Screening Recommendations

  • Screen patients with 2+ first-degree relatives with intracranial aneurysm or SAH (CTA or MRA)
  • Screen patients with autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease (ADPKD)
  • Screen patients with type IV Ehlers-Danlos (vascular subtype) and other connective tissue disorders
  • Repeat screening every 5-7 years for high-risk individuals ages 20-80
  • NASH Score for familial screening: predicts IA risk (5-36%) using Number of affected relatives, Age, Smoking, Hypertension

Key Risk Factors

  • Smoking OR 3.0
  • Hypertension OR 2.9
  • Smoking + hypertension have additive effect on risk
  • Family history of stroke OR 1.6
  • 1 affected family member: ~4% risk. 2+ first-degree relatives: 8-10% risk
  • Female sex, older age, ADPKD, connective tissue disorders, prior SAH
  • Higher rupture risk: >7mm size, posterior circulation, irregular/multilobed morphology

Landmark Evidence
NAIROS RCT: Septoplasty vs. Medical Management (Phase III)
Landmark Phase III RCT
The Nasal AIRway Obstruction Study. Phase III, open-label, multicenter RCT across 17 NHS hospitals. 378 participants randomized to septoplasty (n=188) vs. medical management with intranasal steroid/saline spray (n=190). This is the strongest evidence to date that septoplasty outperforms medical management, and septoplasty is 99-100% likely to be cost-effective at the NHS threshold.
SNOT-22 difference: -20.0 pts
Septoplasty arm: 19.9
Medical arm: 39.5
2024 NIHR HTA →
Septoplasty vs. Non-Surgical: RCT Meta-Analysis
Meta-Analysis
Systematic review of 3 RCTs (721 participants). Septoplasty significantly improved NOSE and SNOT-22 scores at 6 and 12 months vs. non-surgical management, though no notable differences at 3 months. Suggests benefit builds over time. Complication rates: 0.31% revision, 4.12% bleeding/infection.
2024 Springer →
Minimally Invasive Innovations
ClearPath Balloon Septoplasty System
New FDA 510(k) Device
FDA 510(k)-cleared device for patients 17+. Uses a precision-guided balloon catheter with stainless-steel spatula and single-use non-elastic balloon. Tested to withstand up to 10 atm of pressure. Can be performed in-office under local anesthesia. Designed for septal deviations, turbinate hypertrophy, and increasing intranasal space.
Success rate: 80-90%
2025 ClearPath →
Incisionless (No-Knife) Septoplasty
Technique
Office-based procedure under local anesthesia using pressurized inflatable dilators to remodel septal spurs and deflections. HD endoscopic cameras for precise correction. Minimal recovery: mild pain medication for a couple of days, most patients return to work the next day.
2024-2025 Advanced ENT →
Endoscopic Septoplasty with Powered Instrumentation
Technique Review
Microdebrider systems with suction and continuous irrigation for improved visualization. Superior intraoperative visualization, more precise correction of posterior deformities and isolated spurs, and lower complication rates vs. conventional headlight technique. Confirmed in a January 2026 narrative review.
JAN 2026 MDPI Medicina →
Piezosurgery / Ultrasonic Septoplasty
New Technique
Uses ultrasonic vibrations for precise bone and cartilage reshaping with minimal tissue trauma. 2025 innovations include AI-assisted real-time bone mapping and the ability to tailor vibration frequencies to individual patient bone density and nasal structure. Preserves structural integrity better than traditional instrumentation.

Surgical Technique Advances
Modified Extracorporeal Septoplasty (Prospective Study)
Prospective Technique
Improved quality of life and aesthetics with low complications. Modified maneuvers preserve the extracorporeal septoplasty philosophy while reducing technical complexity, decreasing surgical time, and eliminating the need for drills. Keystone preservation reduces risk of dorsal destabilization.
NOSE reduction: -42.0
Residual deviation: 7.4%
Minor perforation: 3.7%

Sling-Bridge Technique in Extracorporeal Septorhinoplasty
New Technique
Novel technique for stabilizing the reconstructed septum during extracorporeal septorhinoplasty. Published in Frontiers in Surgery (2024). Represents an evolution in managing complex septal deformities involving the L-strut.

FDA Cleared Device
Made of polydioxanone, FDA-cleared for nasal valve collapse. Inserted in-office under local anesthesia. Absorbs over ~18 months; collagen construct replaces implant by 24 months. A 2024 claims analysis compared LATERA to surgical repair outcomes.
NOSE score reduction (2yr): 58.2%

3D-Printed PCL Nasal Septal Implants
Device Study
Composite microporous polycaprolactone (PCL) nasal implants via 3D printing demonstrate long-term clinical efficacy and safety. Multicenter study confirmed clinical applicability. PCL degrades over 2-3 years, allowing tissue integration. Patient-specific designs enable precise fit.

Cartilage Tissue Engineering (Autologous Chondrocytes)
Research
Autologous chondrocytes harvested from nasal septum, cultured on collagen matrix for 4 weeks, and reimplanted. Safety and efficacy demonstrated in 100+ individuals. Adipose-derived stem cells in decellularized porcine nasal cartilage scaffolds show high proliferation and chondrogenic differentiation.

Patient-specific scaffolds combining non-resorbable cores with hydrogels loaded with autologous chondrocytes or mesenchymal stem cells. A 2024 Frontiers in Surgery case report documented a pediatric nasal reconstruction using a 3D-printed patient-specific titanium implant.
