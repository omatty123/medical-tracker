Gold-Standard Endovascular Devices
Pipeline Flex with Shield Technology (Medtronic)
Gold Std FDA Approved
Third-generation flow diverter with phosphorylcholine surface modification. The leading device for large/giant and wide-neck aneurysms. Diverts blood away from the aneurysm, promoting gradual thrombosis and vessel healing. Requires dual antiplatelet therapy.
12mo occlusion:77.2%
Safety endpoint:3.2%

Only FDA-approved intrasaccular device. Placed inside the aneurysm sac (not across the parent vessel). Best option for wide-necked bifurcation aneurysms where flow diverters aren't ideal. Does not require long-term dual antiplatelet therapy.

Device

Endovascular Coiling (Platinum Coils)
Gold Std
Bare platinum coils remain the baseline endovascular treatment. Endorsed by the ARISE I consensus as mainstay for ruptured aneurysms over surgical clipping based on robust RCT data. Higher recurrence risk than flow diverters but faster procedure, widely available.
ESTABLISHED ARISE I →
Surgical & Pharmacological
Microsurgical Clipping
Gold Std
Open surgical approach. Remains definitive for aneurysms not amenable to endovascular treatment: MCA bifurcation aneurysms, giant/complex morphology, and cases with significant mass effect. Durable long-term occlusion. Higher procedural morbidity vs. endovascular in selected cases.
ESTABLISHED ARISE I →
Oral Nimodipine (SAH Management)
Gold Std
The only pharmacologic therapy proven to improve outcomes after aneurysmal subarachnoid hemorrhage. Give to all SAH patients unless contraindicated. Reduces delayed cerebral ischemia (DCI). IV milrinone used as rescue for refractory DCI (67% clinical improvement).
STANDARD OF CARE AHA 2023 →
Current Screening & Management Guidelines

Who to Screen

  • 2+ first-degree relatives with intracranial aneurysm or SAH (CTA or MRA)
  • Autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease (ADPKD)
  • Type IV Ehlers-Danlos (vascular subtype) and other connective tissue disorders
  • Repeat every 5-7 years for high-risk individuals ages 20-80
  • NASH Score for familial screening: predicts risk (5-36%) using Number of affected relatives, Age, Smoking, Hypertension

Key Risk Factors

  • Smoking OR 3.0
  • Hypertension OR 2.9
  • Smoking + hypertension have additive effect
  • 1 affected family member: ~4% risk. 2+ first-degree: 8-10%
  • Higher rupture risk: >7mm, posterior circulation, irregular/multilobed morphology

Deviated Septum

Surgical, minimally invasive, and non-surgical approaches

Best Case Now

FDA-approved/cleared, clinically validated, available today

Surgical (Strongest Evidence)
Septoplasty (NAIROS Phase III RCT: Definitive Evidence)
Gold Std Phase III RCT
The NAIROS trial (17 NHS hospitals, 378 patients) is the strongest evidence that septoplasty works. Septoplasty dramatically outperformed medical management (intranasal steroid + saline spray). 99-100% likely to be cost-effective at the NHS threshold. Benefits build over time (significant at 6 and 12 months, not at 3 months).
SNOT-22 septoplasty:19.9
SNOT-22 medical:39.5
Difference:-20.0
2024 LANDMARK NIHR HTA →
Modified Extracorporeal Septoplasty
Technique Prospective
For severe L-strut deformities. The septum is removed, reshaped on a back table, and reimplanted. Modified techniques reduce complexity, eliminate drills, and preserve the keystone area. Low complication rate.
NOSE reduction:-42.0
Residual deviation:7.4%
2024 PMC →
Endoscopic Septoplasty with Powered Instrumentation
Technique
Microdebrider with suction and continuous irrigation. Superior visualization, more precise correction of posterior deformities and isolated spurs, and lower complication rates vs. conventional headlight technique. Available now at many ENT centers.
AVAILABLE NOW MDPI →
Minimally Invasive & In-Office (Available Now)
ClearPath Balloon Septoplasty
FDA 510(k) Device
In-office under local anesthesia. Precision-guided balloon catheter repositions nasal structures without cutting tissue. Tested to 10 atm. Patients 17+. Major advantage: no general anesthesia, no OR time, fast recovery. For mild-to-moderate deviations.
Success rate:80-90%
FDA CLEARED ClearPath →
VivAer Nasal Airway Remodeling
FDA Cleared Device
In-office, low-temperature radiofrequency to remodel nasal valve tissue. Not a septoplasty alternative for severe deviation, but excellent for nasal valve collapse component. The strongest non-surgical outcome data available. 4-year durability confirmed.
Breathing improvement (4yr):96%
Sleep improvement:96%
4-YEAR DATA VivAer →
RhinAer Posterior Nasal Nerve Ablation
FDA Cleared Device
Temperature-controlled radiofrequency targeting the posterior nasal nerve, swell body, and inferior turbinate in a single session. Office-based, local anesthesia. Best for the chronic rhinitis component of nasal obstruction.
NOSE decrease (12mo):65.4%
Responder rate (2yr):88%
2-YEAR DATA RhinAer →
LATERA Absorbable Nasal Implant (Stryker)
FDA Cleared Device
Polydioxanone implant for nasal valve collapse. In-office, local anesthesia. Absorbs over ~18 months; replaced by collagen at 24 months. Can be combined with septoplasty or used alone for lateral wall insufficiency.
NOSE reduction (2yr):58.2%
2-YEAR DATA Stryker →
Medical Management (Non-Surgical)
Standard Medical Management
Baseline
Intranasal corticosteroids (fluticasone, mometasone), saline irrigation, oral/nasal decongestants (short-term), antihistamines for allergic component. Cannot physically correct structural deviation. NAIROS proved these are significantly inferior to septoplasty at 6-12 months, but helpful for mild symptoms or surgical contraindications.
STANDARD OF CARE NYU Langone →

Future

Active trials, emerging techniques, biomaterials, and tissue engineering

Active Head-to-Head Trial
VivAer vs. Septoplasty (Cedars-Sinai, NCT06922955)
Emerging Active Trial
Prospective study comparing VivAer radiofrequency to functional rhinoplasty and septoplasty for nasal airway obstruction, testing non-inferiority. If VivAer proves non-inferior, it would dramatically shift practice toward in-office procedures and away from OR-based septoplasty for many patients.
RECRUITING Cedars-Sinai →
Emerging Techniques
Incisionless (No-Knife) Septoplasty
Emerging Technique
Office-based with pressurized inflatable dilators to remodel septal spurs. HD endoscopic cameras for precision. Most patients return to work next day. Still limited availability and less long-term outcome data than traditional septoplasty.
LIMITED AVAILABILITY Advanced ENT →
Piezosurgery with AI-Assisted Bone Mapping
Emerging AI
Ultrasonic vibrations for precise bone and cartilage reshaping. 2025 innovations add AI-assisted real-time bone mapping that tailors vibration frequencies to individual bone density. Minimal tissue trauma, better structural preservation than traditional instrumentation.

Novel method for stabilizing the reconstructed septum during extracorporeal septorhinoplasty. For complex L-strut deformities. Published Frontiers in Surgery 2024. Needs wider adoption and longer-term outcome data.

Biomaterials & Tissue Engineering
3D-Printed PCL Nasal Implants
Device Multicenter
Patient-specific microporous polycaprolactone implants via 3D printing. Degrades over 2-3 years, allowing tissue integration. Multicenter study confirmed clinical applicability. Not yet widely available but getting closer.

Cartilage Tissue Engineering (Autologous Chondrocytes)
Research
Chondrocytes harvested from nasal septum, cultured on collagen matrix for 4 weeks, reimplanted. Safety demonstrated in 100+ individuals. Adipose-derived stem cells in porcine cartilage scaffolds also promising. Could eliminate donor-site morbidity for complex reconstructions.
2025 RESEARCH PMC →
3D Bioprinting for Nasal Cartilage
Research
Patient-specific scaffolds with hydrogels loaded with autologous chondrocytes or mesenchymal stem cells. A 2024 case report documented pediatric nasal reconstruction with a 3D-printed titanium implant. Still early, but patient-specific cartilage replacement is the long-term vision.
